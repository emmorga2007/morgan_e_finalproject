The Peg-Leg Serial Killer

Middlesex County Courthouse, London, Ontario

The gallows of midlesex county courthouse took the live of 15 people. One well remembered individual was a peg-legged cowboy from Texas, Marion Brown, who was on the run form Texan murder charges. During his time in London, Brown was accused of badly assaulting an officer and then later killing another when confronted. However no witnesses could positively identify brown. It is said that brown declared " another innocent man has been convicted, the fact that no grass shall grow on my grave shall prove my innocence." on may 11, 1899, Brown was put to death by hanging and buried in an unmarked grave on the property, as all executions were during that time. Not long after, his grave was paved over to make parking spaces for the court house, effectively insuring no grass grew on his grave. Reportedly, staff has heard him and his peg-leg walking down the halls.

The Missing Billionaire

The Grand Theater, London, Ontario

The first ever owner of London's Grand Theater was billionaire Ambrose Small who disappeared one day leaving no trace behind that police could follow. He was last seen walking down Younge street in Toronto the he simply vanished. Since his disappearance visitors of the grand theater have seen Small during performances, spectating from the crowd of walking out on the balconies.

The Black Donnelley's

The Donelley Farm and Gravestones, Lucan, Ontario

A family that became famous for their brutal feuding with neighbors and ultimately their murder by an angry, drunken mob of men who called themselves the "Peace Society". In 1880 in a single night, 5 of the family members were murdered at 2 locations and their family barn was burned down. Although 2 trails where held, In the old London Court House, no one was convicted for the killings. The Donelleys are buried in St.Patricks church yard, which is closed from the public after the tombstone was repeatedly vandalized. But, people report seeing figures around the tombstone late at night. Also, a famous occurrence is the "Midnight Lady" who is seen riding down the lane to Donnelleys home ever February 4th. It is also known that Horses will not willingly ride past the Donnelly farm after midnight.

Party Guest or Party Ghost?

Eldon House, London, Ontario

The oldest home in London is the Eldon House, built in 1839 by John Harris, Which today stands as a museum. On May 14, 1841, Harris' daughter Sara invited her sweetheart, lieutenant Wenman Wynniatt, to a dinner part held at the home. Showing up much later then expected, Wynniatt was said to be drenched - despite a clear evening - and completely pale. He brushed past Sara and walked into the home without speaking, when she went after him he had vanished. The next day his body was found in the Thames river, having drowned under mysterious circumstances. The last time he was seen before the party was walking down by the Thames en route. It is said that he has be seen, drowned, roaming about the property. Iteam's with in the house are mysteriously knocked over or disturbed and strange noises and cold touches are felt by guests.

Closed Permanently

The London Asylum for the Insane, London, Ontario

The only original sections of the building still standing today are the Main Examination Building and the Infirmary. In 1870, when it was founded on the then outskirts of London, it housed the insane, the poor, and the "disorderly". Early on, many patients that died during care went unclaimed by prejudice families and were buried in "the poor grounds" of the Local Woodland Cemetery. Electro-convulsive Therapy became the norm in 1944, sending nearly all patients for therapy up to 3 times a day. Dr. Ricard Maurice Burke believed that women's "hysteria" was caused by faulty reproductive organs. Leading Burke to perform regular hysterectomies on his female patients. Closed since 2014, passerby's report hearing calling from the building and shadowy figures walking around the property at dusk. When operational, staff often spoke of unusual noises and shadows.

A Witches Grave

The Old St.Thomas Chruch, St.Thomas,Ontario

In the town of St.Thomas there is a legend about a tall imposing tombstone, surrounded by four post and black as soot. They say a witch, Maria Ermatinger, is buried there and the the stone has been blackened by fire after bursting into flames one night. Also, that her familiar, a black cat, who has lived ever since she was buried it rumored to patrol the graveyard disappearing when people try to come close to it. People are warned that if you stand within the four posts on a night of a full moon, Maria will steal your soul from you.
In reality however, Maria was married to Reverend Maurice Baldwin and came from a wealthy family who gave her a lavish funeral and headstone to match, having died from an infection contracted during childbirth. Her grave and posts are made of white sandstone that have blackened over the years from acid rain. The four post were common of the time, usually connected by a chain to signify wealth. This style of gravesite was common for the time, leading to the existence of many rumoured "witches graves". Despite knowing this, many warn against standing inside of the four posts when the chain is gone.

The Blue Mist Tunnel

The Merritton Tunnel, St.Catharines, Ontario

Considered the most paranormally active site in Ontario, Merritton Tunnel, is the scene of a minor train collision ending in tragedy and a lost graveyard, has accumulated many ghost stories. the stories and sightings range from ghostly crying children, to a ghost dog that roams the entrances. However the blue mist itself is disputed as to weather it is a ghost, or something else entirely. Some saying it is a portal to the other side which attacks soul from areas near by. One such area near by is St. Peter's Cemetery which in the 1920's was flooded to make way for a canal. Resulting in hikers finding human remains on the exposed banks during the dry season's. Roaming souls from the disturbed graves are thought to be wandering the tunnel, looking for the other side. Visitors to this tunnel, which only saw three years of service, report feeling pushed when entering the tunnel, sensing someone walking behind them when no one is there along with sightings of apparitions.

Ghostly B&E

Regional Mental Health Center, St.Thomas, Ontario

Open in 1939, the hospital quickly took in 2,400 patients in its first few years of operation. Commodified during the War as a training base, all patients were relocated. However, during its brief early years, it was a well respected and "revolutionary" care center. After many more years of Mental Health Care, it closed its doors in 2014, sitting lost and abandoned to decay slowly while the land sits for sale. Reports of figures seen in the broken boarded up windows and voices behind heard from inside rose steaply in recent years. Leading residents and online communities to believe the old hospital to be haunted. Unfortunately, it is most likely that the cause of these experiences is the rise in popularity of "urban explorer's" who take pictures and videos of abandoned buildings. However, those who have ventured inside report strange noises, footsteps in distant hallways and whispers. There are no lasting reports from previous staff to cooberate this experiences.

A Haunted Death Mask

Old Oxford Courthouse and Jail, Woodstock, Ontario

There was only ever one man put to death in the town of woodstock. In 1860, a man named Thomas Cooke was convicted of murdering his wife and sent to hang. Nearly 3,400 residents of the town showed up to spectate his public execution and were horrified when his heavy weight resulted in the nose decapitating him instantly. After the execution, a death mask as made, taking a plaster model of his face, and hung by the door to the then prison yard as a mindful sight for the prisoners.
In 1982, it was decided to take the mask down. However, everyday after something bizarre happened, lights would flicker on and off, books and files would be thrown from desk top, and a ghostly apparition was seen rarely. Thinking it best, the staff re-hung the mask and instantly the paranormal activities ceased.

Confusion, Fog, and Death

Drummond Hill Cemetery, Niagara Falls, Ontario

The sight of one of the bloodiest battles of Lundy Lane and the war of 1812. Nearly 2000 men died on the grounds that are no Drummond Hill Cemetery. Soldiers reported the fog combined with the smoke from gunfire and cannons made it unclear who one was shooting at. For 6 hours they fired into the foggy field until the American's retreated.
In present time, people report seeing men dressed in red and blue coats walking through the gravestones, sometimes literally. A common sighting is a red coat solider limping off to battle and sudden dense fogs and the smell of smoke. During a Ghost Tour, a group of three visitors all reported seeing three dark figures behind the headstones in broad daylight. When they shouted for the group to look the figures disappeared into thin air
